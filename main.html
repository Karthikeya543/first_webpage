The Holy Trinity Of Cars

The term "Holy Trinity of Cars" refers to a trio of hypercars that were all released around the same time and are often compared for their groundbreaking technology, design, and performance.

2010s Holy Trinity

The "Holy Trinity" of the 2010s set a new benchmark for hybrid hypercars, showcasing cutting-edge technology, electrification, and staggering performance. Here are the iconic models from that era:

1. Ferrari LaFerrari (2013): Featuring hybrid technology, blending a V12 engine with electric motors.
2. McLaren P1 (2013): A plug-in hybrid with a 3.8L twin-turbo V8, producing 903 horsepower.
3. Porsche 918 Spyder (2013): A plug-in hybrid with a 4.6L V8 and two electric motors, delivering 887 horsepower.
